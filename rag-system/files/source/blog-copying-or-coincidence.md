# Copying or coincidence?

Sometimes we hear about copyright cases in the news. Every man and his
dog can listen to, or look at two works side by side and make a personal
judgement call on whether copying occurred or not. But how does it
really stack up - was it copying or coincidence? When you remember that
there are more than 20 million new songs, more than 3 million new books,
hundreds of commercially released films and umpteen works of art put
into the world every year, we have to recognize that there will be real
instances of overlap, coincidence and similarity.

I have an American film director friend, who has been working on a
script since 2016. Early in 2022, an Australian director released an
indie film that had so many substantial similarities it was uncanny;
from the location, and story, right down to the first name and unusual
ethnicity of the female protagonist. Was it copied? Since we were unable
to establish any situation in which the Australian director would have
had access to the American script, we have to view this a considerable
coincidence.

If my friend releases his film, the Australian director could consider
suing him for copyright infringement, since their film came out first.
But they would have a difficult time proving any copying, since the
American has proof in the form of original script drafts dating back to
2016, and documented pre-production talks from that same time, and many
of the coincidental elements were established at that point.

Can both films be released without legal consequence? Absolutely. If no
copyright infringement has occurred, both works are products of their
creators’ imaginations, and are new and original works in their own
right. As creators, we want to release new, revolutionary, significant
works of art, but it’s not only possible, it’s sometimes probable that
we tap into a stream of creativity that someone else is swimming in. We
are entirely within our rights to produce and release our own work, even
if there’s something similar already out there. Having proof of our
independent development, and being as original as we can be in the
expression of our ideas gives us strong protection.
