# How to respond to staff who question the value of e-reporting

**Question:** Why should our organisation use e-reporting instead of data collection surveys?


**Answer:** E-reporting benefits creators, students, and organisations. For creators, it ensures they are paid for their work because usage is reported accurately and consistently throughout the year. For students, research shows that reading list management software increases engagement in learning. For organisations, e-reporting is less disruptive than periodic data collection surveys and produces more comprehensive data, which in turn enables CLNZ to make more accurate royalty distributions. Many licenceholding organisations are also rightholders themselves, so their staff benefit directly as creators when e-reporting is done well.
