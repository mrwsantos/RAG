# How to get permission to use a copyright work

**Question:** How do I get permission to use a copyright work?


**Answer:** To get permission to use a copyright work, follow these steps. First, confirm the material is still under copyright. If copyright has expired or the work is not eligible for protection, permission is not required. Second, determine exactly which rights you need: how much you want to use, how you plan to use it, for how long, and whether you intend to publish or share it publicly. Third, identify the copyright owner. The creator and the copyright owner may not be the same person, and there can be multiple owners. For published works, contact the publisher first as they are often authorised to grant permission. Collecting societies can also help identify owners. Fourth, make your request in writing, clearly describing yourself, your intended use, and the purpose. If the use is educational or non-commercial, say so. The copyright owner may require a fee or impose conditions. Fifth, follow up if you do not receive a response. Keep records of all communications and any permissions granted. If permission cannot be obtained, consider paraphrasing rather than reproducing the material, reducing the amount used to fall within a fair dealing exception, or where applicable suppressing the material from a published version. Note that acknowledging the source of a work is not a substitute for permission.
