# Use of copyright material in teaching

**Question:** What do teaching staff need to know about using copyright material in their courses?


**Answer:** Teaching staff in the tertiary sector handle copyright material regularly when preparing course packs and delivering lessons. Copyright compliance is both a legal obligation and a professional responsibility. Working in education gives you access to statutory permissions and collective licences that allow more extensive use of copyright material than the general rules would permit. These permissions apply only to material used for educational or instructional purposes, and generally only for enrolled students or staff at the institution. If you intend to use material outside the education context, you will likely need permission from the copyright owner. Attribution is always required for any copyright material copied and distributed during a course, including articles, images, and video clips. Some teaching resources are not protected by copyright at all: material in the public domain (where copyright has expired) and certain public documents such as statutes, court judgements, and official reports do not require permission.
