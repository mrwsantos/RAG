# Proportion of source material that can be copied

**Question:** How much of a work can I copy under the CLNZ licence?


**Answer:** The standard copying limits under the CLNZ licence depend on the type of material. For books organised into chapters, you may copy up to one chapter. For books not divided into chapters, you may copy up to 10% of the work. Both limits include photographs and charts that appear within the selected portion. For books of short stories or poetry, up to 15 pages may be copied. For journals, periodicals, and magazines, you may copy one article per issue. If multiple articles from the same issue are on the same subject, more than one may be copied. For licences that include newspapers, up to five articles per issue may be copied from both local and overseas newspapers. Supporting materials such as references, endnotes, and case studies that appear outside the selected 10% or chapter can also be copied without counting toward the limit, as long as they are directly referred to within the copied section and are part of the same physical work. In some circumstances, CLNZ can approve copying beyond these limits. Contact CLNZ if you are uncertain how these provisions apply to a specific case.
