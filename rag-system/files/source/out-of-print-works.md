# Out of print works

**Question:** Can I copy more than the standard limit from an out of print work?


**Answer:** If a work you want to copy from is no longer available for purchase at a reasonable commercial price, and your organisation holds a copy but in insufficient quantities for teaching or library purposes, you can apply to CLNZ for permission to copy beyond the standard 10% or one chapter limit. To be approved, CLNZ must be able to verify that the work is genuinely unavailable. A work is considered out of print if it is stated as such on a publisher or supplier website, or if it is only available second-hand. If the work is available from another source, in a later edition, or in another format, it is not considered out of print for these purposes, and an application to copy an out of print edition when a later edition exists cannot be approved. Applications are subject to approval and a quote will be provided. This service is available through the MyCopyright Licence Portal, and applies to universities, institutes of technology and polytechnics, wānanga, private training establishments, and primary and secondary schools.
