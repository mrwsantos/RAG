# What are the rights authors have in their work?

**Question:** What is an author's fundamental right in their work?


**Answer:** The author's fundamental right is to decide how their work will be used, subject to some limitations. The law gives authors a set of exclusive rights to do certain activities in relation to their work or to authorise another person to do them. Economic rights are the exclusive rights that provide authors with the potential to earn a living from their creative effort. They include the right to: copy a work; issue copies to the public for the first time; perform, play or show the work in public; communicate the work to the public; and adapt the work. Communicating a work to the public covers broadcasting on radio or TV, transmitting electronically, and making a work available online. Adapting a work includes translating a poem from one language to another, converting a novel into a film script, or doing any of the restricted acts in relation to an adaptation. Anyone who wants to copy or do any of the restricted acts in relation to someone else's copyright material generally needs to ask for permission, unless a copyright exception applies. From a content user's perspective, the exclusive copyright rights are often referred to as 'restricted acts'.
