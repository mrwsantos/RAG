# Internet material

**Question:** What are the copyright rules for using material found on the internet?


**Answer:** You should assume that all material on the internet is under copyright unless you have confirmed otherwise. There is no implied permission to copy online material simply because it is publicly available. Before printing or distributing internet material to students, check the website's terms of use or copyright page. Some material may be available under conditions that permit educational use, such as requiring attribution. If no terms are stated and you cannot identify the copyright owner, seek permission from the website owner. Sharing a URL link is not copying and does not infringe copyright. It is generally not an infringement to view a legitimate live stream, though this depends on whether the stream is authorised. Educational institutions may store webpages for teaching purposes under the Copyright Act, but only if access is restricted through a verification process such as a password, and this exception does not permit printing of webpage content for distribution to students. Open access licences allow copyright material to be used without individual permission, sometimes without payment, but any conditions attached must be followed. If teaching an online course open to the public (such as a MOOC), standard collective or subscription licences cannot be relied upon as they require content to be communicated in a secure environment. Use open access or public domain content where possible, or seek individual permissions.
