# What material can copyright protect?

**Question:** What types of material can copyright protect?


**Answer:** Copyright applies to a broad range of material made available in hard copy or digital form. In New Zealand, the categories include literary, dramatic, musical and artistic works; the typographical arrangement of published editions; sound recordings; films; and communication works such as TV/radio broadcasts and internet transmissions. Copyright exists in a work separately and independently of the tangible object it is contained in. A book, journal or computer database may contain separate stories, articles and software, illustrations, photographs and typographical arrangement --- all of which are capable of copyright protection. A work is the intangible product of an author's creative or intellectual input, expressed in a material form. It is not the physical object --- such as a book or CD --- in which it may be contained.
