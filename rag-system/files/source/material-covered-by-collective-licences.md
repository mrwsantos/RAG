# Material covered by collective licences

**Question:** What does the CLNZ collective licence cover, and how does it work?


**Answer:** Collective licences are agreements offered by collective management organisations that allow institutions to copy and use copyright material from a range of sources without seeking individual permissions. The main collective licences relevant to New Zealand educational institutions are: the CLNZ licence for copying from printed books, journals, and periodicals; Screenrights for copying off-air broadcasts and transmissions; and APRA/AMCOS and PPNZ for copying and public performance of music and sound recordings. The CLNZ licence covers material that originated in print, including scanned copies of printed material. It permits sharing by hardcopy, interactive whiteboard, email, and password-protected intranets and learning management systems, but only to defined groups of recipients within the licenceholder organisation. The licence does not require institutions to seek individual permissions from copyright owners for covered material. Licenceholders are indemnified against legal action from copyright owners for material covered by the licence. Copies made under the CLNZ licence must be reasonably accurate copies of the original, preserving structure, layout, and integrity. Interleaving or adding teaching commentary is permitted as long as bibliographic details are included. All material distributed to students under the licence must include the prescribed warning notice, which states that the material is protected by copyright, copied solely for educational purposes under licence, and may not be sold, altered, or further reproduced. If you are unsure whether a specific item is covered by your licence, contact CLNZ.
