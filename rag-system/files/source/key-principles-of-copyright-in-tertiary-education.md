# Key principles of copyright in tertiary education

**Question:** What are the key principles of copyright that tertiary education staff need to know?


**Answer:** Copyright law in New Zealand is governed by the Copyright Act 1994, which sets out what types of material are protected, who owns copyright by default, and what uses are permitted. In the tertiary education context, the Act governs both what staff and students can do with third-party copyright material and who owns material they create. The default rules under the Act are relatively restrictive when it comes to copying for distribution in class. In practice, these defaults are often extended by licence agreements between educational institutions and copyright owners or their representatives. A CLNZ licence, for example, gives blanket permission to copy and distribute a wide range of printed material that would otherwise require individual permissions. Employment agreements at tertiary institutions may also modify the default copyright ownership rules, for example by specifying that employees retain ownership of certain works they create in the course of employment.
