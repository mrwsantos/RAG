# Copyright in New Zealand

**Question:** How does copyright work in New Zealand?


**Answer:** Copyright in New Zealand is a bundle of exclusive rights that attach automatically to original works once they are written down or recorded. No registration is required. NZ copyright law is established by the Copyright Act 1994 and subsequent court decisions. While NZ law shares similarities with other countries, there are differences in areas such as copyright duration, ownership, and exceptions. When using or distributing material internationally, the law of the country where the material is used generally applies -- seek specific legal advice for international situations.
