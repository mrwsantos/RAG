# Reading list management

**Question:** What is reading list management and why does it matter for CLNZ compliance?


**Answer:** Reading list management systems help tertiary education providers supply copyright compliant course readings to students, whether as printed course packs or electronically. Institutions licensed with CLNZ are required to record what is copied for students under the licence. Reading list management systems, which may be a separate application, an LMS plug-in, or a manual process, are how most institutions meet this requirement. Correct use of these systems ensures that bibliographic information about copied material is available to provide to CLNZ, enabling CLNZ to distribute licence fees to the publishers and authors whose content has been copied. These systems may also record the use of e-books or e-journals from institutional subscriptions. Contact your institution's copyright officer for information about which system your organisation uses.
