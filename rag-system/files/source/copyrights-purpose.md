# Copyright's Purpose

**Question:** Why does copyright exist?


**Answer:** Copyright exists primarily to incentivise the creation and sharing of creative works by giving creators the ability to earn from their work. Society benefits from having access to books, art, music, and other creative works, and copyright helps ensure creators can be rewarded for producing them. Copyright is not absolute. Exceptions exist for educational and research use, and licensing schemes like CLNZ allow institutional copying within defined limits. The aim is to balance creator rights with the public's interest in accessing knowledge.
