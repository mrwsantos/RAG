# What's the difference between licence payments and public lending right payments?

**Question:** What is the difference between a CLNZ licence payment and a public lending right (PLR) payment?


**Answer:** They are entirely different. PLR is a government programme designed to compensate authors and publishers for the lending of their work to a reader at a library. A CLNZ licence payment remunerates for copying that takes place after a work has been purchased --- where it would be inefficient for the licenceholder to purchase multiple copies and similarly inefficient for the rightsholder to administer many permissions requests. The public lending right (PLR) is a government programme designed to compensate authors and publishers for the lending of their work to readers at libraries. A CLNZ licence payment remunerates for copying that takes place after a work has been purchased by the user --- in situations where it would be inefficient for the licenceholder to purchase multiple copies and similarly inefficient for the rightsholder to administer many individual permissions requests.
