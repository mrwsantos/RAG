# What if rights have reverted to the author or the author agreed to the reuse?

**Question:** Does author approval or reversion of rights mean I can reuse copyright material freely?


**Answer:** This depends on the specifics of the author's publishing contract. If the author has given explicit permission and their contract clearly confirms that rights have reverted to them, that permission is likely valid. However, if the contract does not expressly address reversion, or if it requires a specific procedure to occur before rights formally revert, the author may not yet hold the rights they believe they have. In those circumstances, the author should seek legal advice or consult with the publisher before granting permission to others. If you are relying on an author's permission to reuse material, it is worth confirming that they have actually verified their rights position rather than simply assuming reversion has occurred.
