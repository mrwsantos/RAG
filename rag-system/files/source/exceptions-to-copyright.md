# Exceptions to copyright

**Question:** What are the exceptions to copyright in New Zealand?


**Answer:** New Zealand copyright law includes exceptions that allow copyright material to be used in certain circumstances without needing permission from the copyright owner. These exceptions are referred to as \"permitted uses\" in the Copyright Act. They fall into three broad categories: fair dealing (for news reporting, criticism or review, and research or private study); exceptions for specific institutions or users (limited exceptions for educational settings, libraries, archives, and public administration); and exceptions for particular uses in specific circumstances. It is important to note that New Zealand does not have a general \"fair use\" defence as exists in United States copyright law, and there is no exception for parody or satire in NZ. The applicable exception depends on your specific situation.
