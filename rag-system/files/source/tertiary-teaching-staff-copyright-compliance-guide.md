# Tertiary teaching staff copyright compliance guide

**Question:** What am I allowed to copy and share with students under my CLNZ licence and the Copyright Act?


**Answer:** The following summarises what teaching staff at tertiary institutions may do with different types of copyright material. If a proposed use is not covered, permission must be obtained from the copyright owner. For material used in examinations: Under section 49 of the Copyright Act, teaching staff may copy any copyright material for examination purposes, including setting questions, communicating questions to candidates, and answering questions. Assignments, theses, and dissertations that count toward a final grade are treated as examinations for this purpose. For printed books, journals, and periodicals under the CLNZ licence: Teaching staff may photocopy or scan up to 10% of a published work or one chapter, whichever is greater. For journals and periodicals, one whole article per issue may be copied, or more than one article from the same issue if they are closely related and focus on a particular aspect of the same subject. Up to 15 pages of a single work in a published collection may be copied. Copies may be distributed to students in hard copy or electronically via a secure, password-protected system. The same staff member may not make repeat copies of the same material for the same course within 14 days. Excluded from the CLNZ licence: printed music including words, loose maps and charts, free house journals, standalone artworks and photographs, privately owned tuition documents, theses and dissertations, and any work where the copyright owner has prohibited copying. For printed books, journals, and periodicals under section 44 of the Copyright Act (where the CLNZ licence does not apply): Teaching staff may make multiple copies of up to 3% or 3 pages of a work, whichever is greater. If that portion constitutes the whole work (such as a poem), only 50% may be copied. No re-copying of that portion or any other part of the same work is permitted for 14 days. No charges may be made to students. Copies may be communicated electronically to students. For artistic works such as illustrations, photos, diagrams, and graphs: No standalone copying is permitted. Copying is only allowed where the artwork is included on a page being copied under the CLNZ licence or under section 44 of the Copyright Act. For printed or sheet music including lyrics: Excluded from the CLNZ licence. Under section 44, the same 3% or 3-page limit applies. For out of print works under the CLNZ licence: Copying is permitted on a case by case basis with prior written permission from CLNZ. The out of print procedure set out in the licence must be followed. Copies may be supplied in hard copy only. For digital works from library databases: Use is governed by the terms of the relevant subscription licence. Where permitted, multiple copies of up to 3% or 3 pages may be made under section 44. For internet content: Copy, print, and distribute in accordance with any applicable open access licence. Under section 44, up to 3% or 3 pages may be copied. Webpages may be stored for educational purposes under section 44A, provided the page is displayed under a separate frame or identifier, the author and source are identified, the institution name and storage date are stated, access is password protected, and the stored page is deleted when no longer relevant to the course. No printing of stored webpages or their content is permitted. Providing a URL link instead of copying the material avoids copyright risk entirely. For sound recordings, films, and communication works: Under section 45, copies may be made for the educational purpose of making a film or film soundtrack, or for language learning or correspondence lessons. Under section 47, staff and students may perform or show works before an audience of staff and students at the institution only. Paying audiences are not permitted. For commercial music and recordings under the music licence: Teaching staff may perform or play for educational purposes, copy and communicate performances for archival or private use (with performer consent), and combine music with video or digital recordings for educational purposes. Excluded: performance of Grand Rights works (e.g. full theatrical productions), choral works over 20 minutes in their entirety, performances in a dramatic context with costumes and scripted dialogue, and parody or burlesque. For TV and radio broadcasts under the Screenrights licence: Teaching staff may copy from radio and TV broadcasts including Pay TV, download legally available online audiovisual programmes, make multiple copies in any format, use learning management systems to share copied programmes, and retain copied materials as an ongoing educational resource.
