# Infringement of copyright (general)

**Question:** What is copyright infringement?


**Answer:** Copyright is infringed when someone performs a restricted act in relation to copyright material without the owner's permission. The most common ground is copying, which means reproducing or recording a work in any material form: by hand, photocopying, scanning, downloading, or storing on a computer. Copying occurs where all, or a substantial part, of the work is reproduced. A substantial part is any important, distinctive, or essential part, and can be very small in quantity. It may be enough to infringe copyright by reproducing just a few lines of a poem or a paragraph from a journal article, if those parts are significant. Infringement by authorisation can occur where a person sanctions or turns a blind eye to infringing conduct by others. There are also prohibitions on importing infringing copies, commercial dealing with infringing copies, and assisting others to infringe. If your copyright is infringed, it is advisable to seek legal advice. Remedies available through the courts include injunctions, delivery or disposal of infringing copies, damages, and an account of profits. Commercial-scale piracy can attract criminal liability including fines and imprisonment. If your copyright is managed by a collective organisation, notify them of any suspected infringement.
