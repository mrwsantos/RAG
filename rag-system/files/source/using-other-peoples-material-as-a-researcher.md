# Using other people's material as a researcher

**Question:** What do I need to know about using copyright material in my research?


**Answer:** As a tertiary researcher, you rely heavily on copyright material: books, journals, images, and more. You are responsible for using that material within the rules. You have access to copyright privileges through your educational institution that reduce the need for individual permissions in many situations, but these apply only within the educational context. If you are writing a thesis or conducting research likely to be published, take particular care with third party copyright material. Publishing generally takes your use outside the scope of educational exceptions and licences, meaning you may need to seek permission for uses that were previously covered. Regardless of whether you have a legal entitlement to use particular material, you must always acknowledge what you did not create. This means providing full bibliographic citations and, where relevant, attributing illustrators, photographers, and other creators as well as authors.
