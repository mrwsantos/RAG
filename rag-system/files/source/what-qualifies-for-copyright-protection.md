# What qualifies for copyright protection?

**Question:** What does copyright protect --- ideas or expression?


**Answer:** Copyright applies to the material expression of an idea or information, not to the idea itself. Copyright is not intended to give anyone ownership or rights in ideas, information, facts, styles or techniques. No. Once a work is expressed in a material form, such as in writing, it is protected --- even if it has not been published and whether or not it has a copyright notice or symbol attached to it. Yes. Material that is recorded or published on the internet is protected to the same extent as material recorded or published in other forms. Originality in copyright has little to do with literary or artistic quality or merit. It is generally sufficient if the work is original to the author --- meaning the author must have expended skill, judgement or labour sufficient to make the work original. The categories include: literary works (written, spoken or sung material, including computer programs and tables); dramatic works (plays, scripts, film scenarios); artistic works (paintings, drawings, photographs, maps); musical works (scores and sheet music); typographical works; sound recordings; films; and communication works such as broadcasts and internet transmissions. Yes. A published edition may include separate copyright in the literary work or works it contains, in the typographical arrangement of the published edition, and in any artistic works such as illustrations or photographs. Each work may have a different copyright owner and a different copyright duration.
