# Own works

**Question:** Do I need permission to copy my own copyright material?


**Answer:** Generally, if you own the copyright in material you created, you do not need permission to copy it. However, you may not automatically own the copyright just because you created it. If you created material in the course of employment, your employer may own the copyright or may have imposed conditions on its use. For teachers, material created with Ministry of Education resources or under a research contract may be subject to restrictions, though it may sometimes be available under an open access licence. If you have published your own work, any assignment or licence you granted to the publisher may limit what you can do with it. You should check your employment agreement and any publishing agreements before assuming you can use your own work freely.
