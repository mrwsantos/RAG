# If a work is available online, is it public domain?

**Question:** Does copyright apply to material that is freely available online?


**Answer:** No. Copyright applies regardless of whether a work is available online. Being publicly accessible is not the same as being in the public domain. The copyright owner still controls how their work is used, and their rules will usually be stated in the website terms of use or copyright notice. Note also that material you find online may not have been placed there by the copyright owner. A work only enters the public domain once copyright expires, which in New Zealand is 50 years after the death of the author. Most works available online are still under copyright.
