# What happens after data collection?

**Question:** What happens to my organisation's data after it is supplied to CLNZ?


**Answer:** Once your data is supplied to CLNZ, along with data from other participating organisations, it is processed by CLNZ's in-house research staff. Their work involves researching and finding the correct owner of the work that has been copied. It is important to include all the information requested because CLNZ's research staff need this to identify and find the correct copyright owner of the work that has been copied. Once processed, financial distribution to authors and creators becomes possible. Yes. At the completion of the data collection, CLNZ will share information on the outcome of the process with the copyright liaison person, who will be encouraged to share it with the rest of their team, so everyone can see the contribution they have made to authors and creators.
