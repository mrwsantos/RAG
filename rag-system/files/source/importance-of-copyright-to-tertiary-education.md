# Importance of copyright to tertiary education

**Question:** Why does copyright matter in tertiary education?


**Answer:** Almost all information resources used and created in tertiary education are protected by copyright, including textbooks, journal articles, course packs, lecture notes, images, audio and video, software, and website material. Copyright matters because it encourages creators to invest in producing quality learning materials, while also balancing society's interest in access and sharing. Teachers and students are often both creators and users of copyright material, so understanding copyright protects their own work as well as ensuring they use others' work legally. Attribution is required in many situations including under moral rights, fair dealing, and educational copying provisions, and is good academic practice regardless. It is important to note that acknowledging a source does not substitute for permission where permission is legally required.
