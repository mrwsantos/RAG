# Permissions checklist

**Question:** What steps should I follow to check whether I need permission to use copyright material?


**Answer:** Use the following checklist when you want to use third party content and are unsure whether permission is required. First, check whether the material qualifies for copyright protection. Ideas, information, names, titles, and short standalone texts are generally not protected. Second, if the material is protected, check whether permission is still needed. Consider: are you using less than a substantial part? Has copyright expired? Does a statutory exception apply to your use, such as fair dealing? Does an existing licence you hold cover this use? Third, if permission is required: identify the copyright owner, noting that the creator and the owner may not be the same person. For published works, contact the publisher first. Collective management organisations and professional associations can also help identify owners. For older works with unknown authors, consider whether it is reasonable to assume copyright has expired. Determine exactly which rights you need. Make your request in writing. Keep a record of all communications.
