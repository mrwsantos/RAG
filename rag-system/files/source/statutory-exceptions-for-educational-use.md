# Statutory exceptions for educational use

**Question:** What statutory copyright exceptions apply if my institution does not hold a CLNZ licence?


**Answer:** The Copyright Act contains educational exceptions that allow certain copying and sharing of copyright material for educational purposes. However, these statutory provisions, particularly those relating to copying print material for students, are quite restrictive. For most tertiary education providers, collective and subscription licences have superseded the statutory defaults by allowing significantly more use than the Act permits alone. If your institution does not hold a relevant licence, or your requirements fall outside the scope of your licence, the statutory exceptions may still apply to some uses. For specific details, refer to the Copyright Act 1994 or consult your institution's copyright officer.
