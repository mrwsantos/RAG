# How long does copyright last?

**Question:** How long does copyright last in New Zealand?


**Answer:** In New Zealand, copyright in literary, dramatic, musical, and artistic works lasts for the life of the author plus 50 years from the end of the year they died. For jointly authored works, the 50 years runs from the death of the last surviving author. For typographical arrangements of published editions, the term is 25 years from publication. For Crown copyright, most works are protected for 100 years from the end of the year the work was made, with typographical arrangements protected for 25 years. Where authorship is unknown, copyright expires 50 years after the end of the year the work was first made available to the public by an authorised act. For unpublished works created before the Copyright Act 1994 came into force, a special term of life of the author plus 75 years applies if the author died before the end of 1994. Note that copyright terms in Australia, Europe, and the United States are generally longer at life plus 70 years, so a work in the public domain in New Zealand may still be protected in those countries.
