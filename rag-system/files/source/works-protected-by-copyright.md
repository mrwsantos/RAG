# Works protected by copyright

**Question:** What types of material are protected by copyright in the tertiary education context?


**Answer:** The vast majority of material used in the tertiary education sector is capable of copyright protection. Copyright protects most original material intended to entertain or convey information, regardless of artistic or literary merit. This includes written content, musical compositions, artworks, published design layout, audio and video clips, broadcasts, and webcasts. A literary work means any work, other than a dramatic or musical work, that is written, spoken, or sung --- such as the words of a book, poem, newspaper or journal article, speech or song. It also includes tables, compilations, and computer programs. A dramatic work includes a play, a work of dance or mime, and a film scenario or script. A musical work means a musical composition such as a score or sheet music, but does not include any lyrics or sound recording. An artistic work includes graphic works such as paintings, drawings, diagrams, maps, charts, plans, engravings, etchings, lithographs, woodcuts and similar works; photographs; sculptures; collages; models; works of architecture; and works of artistic craftsmanship. It does not include layout designs or integrated circuits within the meaning of the Layout Designs Act 1994. A typographical arrangement refers to the arrangement or appearance of words and associated elements in published editions --- such as books, journals, newspapers, posters and websites --- separate from the underlying works they contain. Published edition means a published edition of the whole or any part of one or more literary, dramatic, or musical works. A sound recording means a recording of sounds from which sounds may be reproduced --- such as on CDs and audio files --- separate from the underlying music or other works the recording contains. A film means a recording on any medium from which moving images may be produced --- such as on video or a DVD --- separate from the underlying works it contains, such as scripts and music. A communication work means a transmission of sounds, visual images or other information for reception by members of the public. This includes a radio or television broadcast or internet transmission, separate from the underlying works it contains such as scripts, music and films. Yes. There can be more than one type of copyright work in a single resource. For example, a textbook may have separate copyright in the literary work, the typographical arrangement, and any artistic works such as illustrations. A DVD or video clip may have separate copyright in the film, the dramatic work (screenplay), the musical composition, and the sound recording. Each work may have a different copyright owner and a different copyright duration. Yes. A single resource can contain a mixture of public domain works (material in which copyright has expired) and works that are still protected by copyright. There can also be different copyright owners applicable within a single resource.
