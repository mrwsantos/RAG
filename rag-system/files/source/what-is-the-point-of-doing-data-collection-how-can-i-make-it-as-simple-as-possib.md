# What is the point of doing data collection? How can I make it as simple as possible?

**Question:** Why does CLNZ collect data on copying from licenceholders?


**Answer:** Data collection provides CLNZ with usage data used to distribute funds to the rightsholders who have agreed to allow CLNZ to licence their material. Without their support, licenceholders would have to go through a complex compliance process for each individual copying act. CLNZ will simply notify the organisation of the issue. It is then up to the organisation to decide how to remedy it. CLNZ is not the copyright police and it is not their intention to take punitive actions in response to data supplied. Practical steps include: communicating the purpose of data collection to staff; encouraging staff to maintain a bibliography of all course materials used; treating copyright as a compliance issue addressed during new staff induction and professional development; and asking CLNZ about best practice from other licenceholders. Once data is supplied, CLNZ's in-house research staff process it alongside data from other participating organisations. Their work involves researching and finding the correct owner of the work that has been copied, enabling financial distribution to authors and creators whose works are shown to be copied. Yes. At the completion of the data collection, CLNZ shares information on the outcome of the process with the copyright liaison person, who is encouraged to share it with the rest of their team, so everyone can see the contribution they have made to New Zealand authors and creators.
