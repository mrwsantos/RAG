# How to stay copyright compliant

**Question:** How do I make sure I am using copyright material correctly?


**Answer:** The starting point is to identify the source of the material you want to use, as this determines what permissions may already exist. If your organisation holds a CLNZ licence, you may have permission to copy certain printed material, off-air recordings, and music. Without a licence, more limited copying may still be possible under the educational provisions of the Copyright Act. For material from an electronic database your institution subscribes to, the terms of that subscription licence apply. For material found online, check whether it is available under an open licence such as Creative Commons. If none of these permissions apply, you will need to seek permission directly from the copyright owner. Your librarian or copyright officer can assist with this.
